Condition-Level Data Sources & Methodology Notes

CDC Chronic Disease Fast Facts (2024): Heart disease & stroke ($233.3B direct + $184.6B lost productivity), diabetes ($413B total burden 2022), obesity ($173B annual healthcare cost), CKD ($95.7B Medicare spending). URL: cdc.gov/chronic-disease/data-research/facts-stats
National Cancer Institute — Cancer Statistics (2024): Cancer total national expenditure $208.9B (2020 reference year); 18.1M cancer survivors as of Jan 2022; projected 2M+ new cases in 2025. URL: cancer.gov/about-cancer/understanding/statistics
CDC Stroke Data & Statistics: Stroke-related costs $56.2B (2019–2020 two-year combined), 795,000+ annual stroke cases. URL: cdc.gov/stroke/data-research/facts-stats
CDC Hypertension FastStats (NCHS): 49.1% of U.S. adults with hypertension; 122M+ adults affected. Hypertension cost estimate ($131B) derived from CMS Chronic Conditions Data Warehouse per-beneficiary spending benchmarks and AHRQ MEPS condition-level expenditure reports.
CMS Chronic Conditions Data Warehouse: Per-beneficiary Medicare spending by condition; payer mix benchmarks for Medicare-predominant conditions (CKD, heart disease, COPD). URL: cms.gov/Research-Statistics-Data-and-Systems/Statistics-Trends-and-Reports/ChronicConditions
AHRQ Medical Expenditure Panel Survey (MEPS): Per-patient expenditure benchmarks for musculoskeletal/arthritis, mental health, and outpatient-heavy conditions. Payer mix proportions for private-insurance-predominant conditions. URL: ahrq.gov/data/meps
CDC COPD About Page: 16M diagnosed U.S. adults with COPD. COPD direct medical cost estimate ($50B) derived from CMS Chronic Conditions per-beneficiary spending and AHRQ MEPS cost-of-condition estimates.
Alzheimer's Association / HHS (2024): Alzheimer's & dementia total care cost $360B (2024), nearly 7M Americans affected. Included in mental health/behavioral category alongside NIMH mental illness prevalence data (57.8M adults with any mental illness per NIMH 2021).
Important methodology note: Condition-level spending figures span reference years 2019–2024 due to varying data publication cycles across government sources. Dollar figures represent direct medical costs unless otherwise noted; indirect costs (lost productivity) are excluded from chart values to ensure cross-condition comparability. Per-patient costs are derived by dividing total direct costs by estimated prevalence. Payer mix percentages are modeled estimates based on CMS program enrollment by condition, AHRQ MEPS insurance type tabulations, and CMS Medicare/Medicaid chronic condition data. All figures should be treated as approximations for portfolio analytical purposes, not actuarial pricing inputs.
Data Sources & Methodology: All primary NHE data sourced from the CMS National Health Expenditure Accounts (NHEA), published by the CMS Office of the Actuary. 2024 figures are from the CMS NHE Fact Sheet (released 2025). Historical values for 2014–2023 are derived from CMS NHEA historical tables consistent with published CMS data series. Per capita figures use U.S. Census Bureau resident population estimates consistent with CMS methodology. GDP shares use Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA) nominal GDP consistent with CMS reporting.
